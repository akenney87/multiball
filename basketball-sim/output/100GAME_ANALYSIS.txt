================================================================================
KEY INSIGHT: TEAM COMPOSITION MATTERS
================================================================================

The massive variance between Phase 4 and 100-game validation reveals that
shot distribution is HEAVILY influenced by team composition. This is actually
REALISTIC and desirable behavior!

In the NBA:
  - Warriors (elite shooters): 45%+ three-point attempts
  - Bucks (dominant big): 38%+ rim attempts
  - Different teams have different shot distributions based on personnel

What we observed:
  - Specialist teams (Alpha/Beta): 43.5% 3PT, 36.9% rim
  - Diverse teams (average): 38.4% 3PT, 28.9% rim
  - 5-8% variance based on team composition

This means the system is WORKING AS INTENDED - shot distribution responds to
team attributes and composition. However, we want the AVERAGE across all teams
to align with NBA averages (40% 3PT, 25% mid, 35% rim).

ROOT CAUSE:
  Phase 4 baseline adjustments pushed rim too low:
    - Baseline: 28% (was 30% in Phase 3c)
    - Combined with Phase 3c composite bonus reduction (0.008 -> 0.005)
    - Diverse teams attack rim even less than specialist teams

SOLUTION:
  Increase rim baseline: 28% -> 32-33%
  This will bring average closer to 35% target

ISSUE 3: 3PT Nearly Perfect (38.4% vs 40% target)
  - 100-game average: 38.4%
  - NBA target: 40.0%
  - Variance: -1.6% (EXCELLENT!)

ROOT CAUSE:
  Phase 4 baseline (40%) works well for diverse teams
  Specialist teams shot higher (43.5%) due to their composition

SOLUTION:
  Keep 3PT baseline at 40% OR increase slightly to 41% to center at 40%

WHY PHASE 4 VALIDATION WAS MISLEADING
================================================================================

Phase 4 used "specialist teams" (Team Alpha/Beta) with specific compositions:
  - Chris Maestro: Elite playmaker (shot creation 83.1)
  - Ray Sniper: Elite shooter
  - Marcus Slasher: Rim attacker
  - Hassan Tower: Traditional center (low shot creation)

These teams have:
  - Higher shot creation scores (more skilled ball handlers)
  - More shooting specialists
  - More balanced versatility

Result: They took MORE 3PTs (43.5%) and MORE rim attempts (36.9%)
        than average teams, while taking LESS midrange (19.5%)

This is NBA-realistic (Warriors/Celtics with elite personnel), but NOT
representative of average team shot distribution.

LESSON LEARNED:
  Validation must use DIVERSE teams to get accurate average distributions.
  Single team-pair validation can be misleading if teams are non-representative.

================================================================================
ARCHITECTURAL IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================

VALIDATION METHODOLOGY:
  Going forward, ALL validation should use:
    1. Large sample size (100+ games)
    2. Diverse team selection (80+ different teams)
    3. Random matchups (avoid same team pairs repeatedly)

  Single team-pair validation (like Phase 4) is useful for:
    - Quick iteration testing
    - Debugging specific scenarios
    - But NOT for final validation of NBA statistical accuracy

BASELINE vs MODIFIERS:
  The baseline distribution (currently 40/32/28) determines the CENTER
  point around which team-specific modifiers operate:
    - Player composites: +/- 5-10%
    - Position modifiers: +/- 3-5%
    - Tactical settings: +/- 5-10%

  We need baselines set so that the AVERAGE across diverse teams matches
  NBA targets, not that ANY SPECIFIC team matches targets.

TEAM COMPOSITION REALISM:
  The fact that different teams produce different shot distributions is
  a STRENGTH of the system, not a weakness. This aligns with:
    - Pillar 1: Deep, intricate, realistic simulation
    - Pillar 3: Attribute-driven outcomes
    - Pillar 4: Tactical input system (team construction affects outcomes)
