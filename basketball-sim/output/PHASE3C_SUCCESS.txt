The key was addressing BOTH who gets the ball AND what shots they take:

  - Jerry (80.9 creation): Gets ~19% usage (increased from ~17%)
  - Chris (83.1 creation): Gets 30% usage (scoring option)

COMPOSITE REDUCTION (What shots they take):
  - Hassan (77.7 rim): Still takes 85-90% at rim (realistic for his role)
  - Jerry (72.5 rim): Now takes ~50% at rim (down from 60%)
  - Vince (81.5 rim): Now takes ~60% at rim (down from 75%)
  - Marcus (78.0 rim): Now takes ~65% at rim (down from 75%)

NET EFFECT:
  - Hassan gets ball less often (shot creation)
  - When he has it, he still attacks rim (realistic)
  - But OTHER players (Jerry, Vince, Marcus) attack rim less frequently
  - Combined: 7.8% rim reduction

THIS IS NBA-REALISTIC:
  - Traditional centers (Hassan, Rudy Gobert) get limited touches
  - They take high % at rim when they do get ball
  - Guards/wings create more offense
  - But they take balanced shot distributions (not rim-heavy)

================================================================================
LESSONS LEARNED
================================================================================

1. USAGE ALONE IS INSUFFICIENT
   - Three attempts at usage distribution (versatility, linear, exponential)
   - All failed to significantly reduce rim attempts
   - Root cause: shot SELECTION, not usage

2. SHOT SELECTION IS THE CORE ISSUE
   - Composite bonuses determine what shots players take
   - This is the primary lever for shot distribution
   - Usage distribution is secondary (but still important)

3. COMBINED APPROACHES WORK BEST
   - Neither usage nor selection alone solves the problem
   - Together they create realistic, attribute-driven outcomes
   - Preserves player identity while achieving NBA distributions

4. USER INTUITION WAS CORRECT
   - "Hassan should take 90% at rim (realistic)"
   - "But he should get ball less often (can't create)"
   - Combined approach implements this exactly
   - User feedback improved the architecture

5. ITERATION IS ESSENTIAL
   - Phase 3a (versatility): Failed (+1.9% rim)
   - Phase 3b (exponential): Failed (+1.0% rim)
   - Phase 3c (combined): Success (-7.8% rim)
   - Each failure informed the solution
