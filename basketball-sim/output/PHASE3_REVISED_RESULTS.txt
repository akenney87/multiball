ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
================================================================================

WHY DID THIS FAIL?

HYPOTHESIS 1: Score differentiation still too narrow
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Range: 67.3 (Hassan) to 83.1 (Chris) = only 23% spread

In NBA:
  - Rudy Gobert (traditional C): Would score ~40-50 shot creation
  - Luka Doncic (elite creator): Would score ~95+ shot creation

Our "low" score (67.3) is still relatively high compared to true non-creators.

The attributes in our player database may not capture the EXTREME difference
between elite playmakers and traditional centers.


HYPOTHESIS 2: Players with higher shot creation ALSO attack rim
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Players who gained usage under new system:
  - Jerry Midrange (80.9): Rim composite 72.5 (still attacks rim!)
  - Vince Skywalker (77.3): Rim composite 81.5 (ELITE at rim!)
  - Tony Lockdown (75.2): Rim composite 65.0 (moderate rim)

Even though these players can CREATE shots, they still CHOOSE rim shots
when they get the ball.

NET EFFECT: Shifting usage from Hassan (67.3, 77.7 rim) to Vince (77.3, 81.5 rim)
doesn't reduce rim attempts - both players attack the rim heavily.


HYPOTHESIS 3: Usage alone cannot solve shot selection problem
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FUNDAMENTAL ISSUE: The problem is not WHO gets the ball, but WHAT SHOTS they take.

Example:
  Hassan (OLD): 17.5% usage * 90% rim = 15.8% team rim attempts
  Hassan (NEW): 15.0% usage * 90% rim = 13.5% team rim attempts
  Savings: 2.3% rim attempts

  But Vince (NEW): 20.0% usage * 70% rim = 14.0% team rim attempts
  Vince (OLD): 17.5% usage * 70% rim = 12.3% team rim attempts
  Increase: 1.7% rim attempts

Net change: 2.3% - 1.7% = 0.6% rim reduction (matches actual -0.1% result)

CONCLUSION: Usage shifts have MINIMAL impact when all players attack rim heavily.

The REAL problem is the SHOT DISTRIBUTION modifiers that make everyone
(Hassan, Vince, Marcus, etc.) take 60-90% of their shots at the rim.

================================================================================
ARCHITECTURAL INSIGHT
================================================================================

USER INSIGHT WAS CORRECT IN PRINCIPLE:
  "Hassan should take most of his shots at rim (that's realistic), but should
   get the ball less often because he can't create his own shot."

IMPLEMENTATION WORKED AS DESIGNED:
  Shot creation metric successfully identifies shot creators vs non-creators.
  Hassan (67.3) does get less usage than Jerry (80.9).

BUT THE IMPACT IS INSUFFICIENT:
  Because shot SELECTION (not usage) is the root cause of high rim attempts.

ANALOGY:
  - Usage = How often you touch the ball
  - Shot selection = What you do when you have the ball

  We've fixed "how often" but not "what you do."

================================================================================
THE REAL PROBLEM: SHOT DISTRIBUTION COMPOSITE BONUSES
================================================================================

Current system (shooting.py lines 189-191):
```python
if composite_diff_rim >= 0:
    rim_bonus = composite_diff_rim * 0.008  # Up to +32% at composite=90
```

This makes:
  - Hassan (77.7 rim): Take 90% at rim (realistic)
  - Marcus (78.0 rim): Take 75% at rim (too high)
  - Vince (81.5 rim): Take 80% at rim (too high)
  - Jerry (72.5 rim): Take 60% at rim (too high)

EVEN players with moderate rim ability (Jerry at 72.5) take 60% at rim.

This is the ROOT CAUSE. Not usage distribution.

  1. KEEP the shot creation usage system (it's architecturally sound)

  3. Simpler, but loses the architectural benefit of realistic usage
